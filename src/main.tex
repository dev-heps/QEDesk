\section{Images and Preimages}

\subsection*{Problem}

Let $f : A \to B$ be a function and let $B_1 \subseteq B$. Prove that
\[
  f\bigl(f^{-1}(B_1)\bigr) \subseteq B_1.
\]

\subsection*{Proof Blueprint}

% QEDesk[node=image_membership][kind=definition][title=Membership in an image][status=open]
For a set $S \subseteq A$, the assertion $b \in f(S)$ means that there is
some $a \in S$ such that $f(a)=b$.

% QEDesk[node=preimage_membership][kind=definition][title=Membership in a preimage][status=open]
The assertion $a \in f^{-1}(B_1)$ means exactly that $f(a) \in B_1$.

% QEDesk[node=mem_of_mem_image_preimage][kind=lemma][title=Element chase through image and preimage][lean=mem_of_mem_image_preimage][status=checked]
% QEDesk[uses=image_membership,preimage_membership]
If $b \in f(f^{-1}(B_1))$, then $b \in B_1$.

% QEDesk[node=image_preimage_subset][kind=theorem][title=Image of a preimage is contained in the target set][lean=image_preimage_subset][status=checked]
% QEDesk[uses=mem_of_mem_image_preimage]
The elementwise lemma proves the subset statement
$f(f^{-1}(B_1)) \subseteq B_1$.

\subsection*{Proof Idea}

To prove a subset relation, start with an arbitrary element of the left-hand
side and prove that it belongs to the right-hand side.

Suppose $b \in f(f^{-1}(B_1))$. Then there exists some $a \in A$ such that
\[
  a \in f^{-1}(B_1)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  f(a) = b.
\]
Since $a \in f^{-1}(B_1)$, by the definition of preimage we have
$f(a) \in B_1$. Since $f(a)=b$, it follows that $b \in B_1$.

Therefore,
\[
  f(f^{-1}(B_1)) \subseteq B_1.
\]
